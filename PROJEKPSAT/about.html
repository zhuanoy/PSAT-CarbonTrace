🌐 About CarbonTrace

A carbon footprint calculator built for the next generation

🌿

What is CarbonTrace?

CarbonTrace is a web-based carbon footprint calculator and environmental education platform designed for Indonesian students and the general public. Developed as a final project for the Software Engineering course, it integrates knowledge from various subjects including Mathematics, History, Physical Education, Indonesian Language, Religion, and Local Content (Javanese Language).

The name "CarbonTrace" comes from two words: Carbon (which refers to carbon emissions that contribute to climate change) and Trace (which means to track or monitor). Together, CarbonTrace means tracking and understanding carbon footprints, helping users measure their daily environmental impact and encouraging more sustainable lifestyle choices to reduce carbon emissions and protect the environment.

🎯

Our Mission

To provide an accessible, educational, and user-friendly platform that empowers Indonesian youth to understand, measure, and reduce their carbon footprint through daily lifestyle changes.

🔭

Our Vision

A generation of environmentally conscious Indonesian citizens who make data-driven decisions about their lifestyle choices, contributing to Indonesia's net-zero emission target by 2060.

📖 How to Use CarbonTrace

1

Navigate the Carbon Calculator

Go to the Calculator page and enter your daily electricity consumption (in kWh), weekly fuel consumption (in liters), and daily travel distance (in km). Click "Calculate" to see your carbon footprint results instantly.

2

Understand Your Results

Your results will show your total CO₂ emissions in kilograms per day, broken down by category (electricity, fuel, and distance). You'll also receive a rating: Low (<10 kg CO₂), Medium (10–30 kg CO₂), or High (>30 kg CO₂).

3

Save and Track Your History

Click "Save History" to store your calculation results locally in your browser. This allows you to track your carbon footprint over time and see whether your efforts to reduce emissions are working.

4

Learn and Get Inspired

Explore other sections: learn about the History of Carbon Emissions, get Health Tips for eco-friendly activities, read Biographies of environmental heroes, and reflect on the Moral Messages about environmental stewardship.

5

Toggle Language & Dark Mode

Use the 🌐 button in the navigation bar to switch between Bahasa Indonesia and English. Use the 🌙 button to toggle between light and dark mode for comfortable reading in any environment.

📚 Key Vocabulary / Kamus Istilah

Understanding the terminology used in carbon footprint calculation is essential for interpreting your results correctly.

Bahasa Indonesia English Definition
Jejak Karbon Carbon Footprint The total amount of greenhouse gases generated by human activities, expressed as CO₂ equivalent.
Emisi Emission The release of gases or particles into the atmosphere, particularly greenhouse gases like CO₂.
Energi Energy The capacity to do work; in this context, electrical energy measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh).
Lingkungan Environment The natural world, including the air, water, land, plants, and animals that surround us.
Perhitungan Calculation The process of using mathematical formulas to determine a numerical result.
Gas Rumah Kaca Greenhouse Gas Gases that trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, including CO₂, methane, and water vapor.
Perubahan Iklim Climate Change Long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns, largely driven by human activities.
Energi Terbarukan Renewable Energy Energy from sources naturally replenished on a human timescale, such as solar, wind, and hydro.
Net Zero Net Zero A state where the amount of greenhouse gas emissions produced equals the amount removed from the atmosphere.
Bahan Bakar Fosil Fossil Fuel Natural fuels such as coal, oil, and gas, formed from the remains of ancient organisms.
Deforestasi Deforestation The large-scale removal of forests, often resulting in damage to ecosystems and increased CO₂ levels.
Emisi Faktor Emission Factor A coefficient representing the average emission rate of a given pollutant per unit of activity.
